Hampi
✦ UNESCO World Heritage Site

Where Boulders
Tell Stories

Hampi is not just a place — it's a fever dream. Massive granite boulders stack impossibly against each other while temple gopurams pierce the skyline. The Tungabhadra winds through, and somewhere between a banana pancake at a rooftop cafe and watching the sunset from Matanga Hill, you lose all track of time.

Once the capital of the mighty Vijayanagara Empire, today it hosts backpackers, hippies, history nerds and every kind of soul in between. There is nowhere else quite like it.

A Brief History

1336 CE
The Vijayanagara Empire is Founded
Harihara I and Bukka Raya I establish the empire near the sacred Tungabhadra river, guided by the sage Vidyaranya. Hampi (then called Vijayanagara — "City of Victory") becomes the capital.
1400s
Golden Age — One of the World's Richest Cities
At its peak, Vijayanagara had over 500,000 inhabitants — making it one of the largest cities in the medieval world. Merchants from Persia, Portugal, and China filled the bazaars. The Vittala Temple complex was built during this era.
1565 CE
The Battle of Talikota — Fall of an Empire
A coalition of Deccan sultanates defeats the empire at the Battle of Talikota. The city is sacked and burned for six months, reducing one of the world's greatest cities to the ruins we see today.
1986
UNESCO World Heritage Status
The ruins of Hampi are officially recognised as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, beginning an era of international attention and preservation. Today over 4,100 monuments span the 4,100-hectare site.
Goa
✦ India's Beach Paradise

Sun, Sand &
Soul Food

Goa is many things to many people. For some, it's the thumping clubs of Anjuna. For others, it's a sleepy hammock in Patnem with a Kingfisher and nothing to do. The Portuguese left behind pastel-painted churches and vindaloo. The hippies left behind flea markets and trance music.

What Goa really is? A mood. And the mood is good.

Cafes, Bars & Beach Shacks

Maza Kai
Beach Shack · Konkani Soul · Anjuna
One of North Goa's most beloved little spots. Maza Kai means "What Fun?" in Konkani — and it delivers. Local seafood, cold beers, sunset views and none of that touristy jazz. Order the prawn balchão and a cold Kings.
SeafoodNorth GoaBeach ViewsLocal Fave
Buddha Tree
Rooftop Jungle · Vegan Haven · Arambol
Nestled in the jungles near Arambol, Buddha Tree is a multi-level rooftop space draped in fairy lights and actual trees. An eclectic menu of Asian-fusion, wood-fired pizzas and the best acai bowl north of the equator. Pure magic after dark.

Babka
European Bakery · Panjim · Morning Gold
A gorgeous little European-style bakery in Panjim's Latin Quarter. Sourdough loaves, proper croissants, cardamom rolls that will ruin you for all other croissants. The cortado is made with single-origin beans. Come early — everything sells out by 10am.

Infantaria
Calangute Legend · Bakery + Café
The oldest bakery in North Goa. Generations of Goans grew up on Infantaria's bebinca (the local layered coconut cake) and egg puffs. Still run by the same family. Pull up a chair, order the bebinca and a lime soda. Time stops here.

Riverside · Seafood · Old Goa
Set on the banks of the Sal River, Fisherman's Wharf does Goan seafood the right way — crab xec xec, fish recheado, prawn curry rice. Wooden interiors, rustic Goan decor and staff who'll tell you what's freshest today.

Curlies has been a Anjuna institution since the '80s rave era. Today it's a chilled beach shack with DJs after dark, a massive menu and the best view of the Anjuna sunset. The rum feni cocktails are dangerously good.

A Brief History

3rd Century BCE
Mauryan Empire — Ancient Roots
Goa (then called Gove or Govapuri) was part of the Mauryan Empire under Ashoka. It later passed through the hands of the Satavahanas, Chalukyas, Rashtrakutas and the Kadamba dynasty who made it a major port city.
1510 CE
Portuguese Conquest — A New Era
Alfonso de Albuquerque captures Goa from the Bijapur Sultanate, beginning 451 years of Portuguese rule. The Portuguese build magnificent churches, introduce Catholicism and create a unique Indo-Portuguese culture that persists today in Goa's architecture, cuisine and family names.
1961
Liberation — India Reclaims Goa
Operation Vijay: The Indian Army marches into Goa, ending Portuguese colonial rule in just 36 hours. Goa becomes part of India, first as a Union Territory and then as India's smallest state in 1987.
1970s–80s
Hippie Trail & the Birth of Goa Trance
International hippies discover Goa's beaches and cheap living. Anjuna and Calangute become hubs for a counterculture movement. All-night beach parties evolve into the Goa Trance music genre — a unique psychedelic electronic music style that influences global dance music culture.
Udaipur
✦ Venice of the East

Lakes, Palaces &
Royal Dreams

Udaipur is Rajasthan's most romantic city — and it knows it. Lake Pichola reflects the City Palace at dusk in colours that no Instagram filter can replicate. Narrow old-city lanes smell of marigolds and incense. Rooftop restaurants compete to offer the best view of the lake.

The Mewar royals built this city with poetry in their blueprints. Every corner has a story. Every sunset makes you feel like you're in a dream sequence.

A Brief History

1559 CE
Maharana Udai Singh II Founds Udaipur
After the fall of Chittorgarh to Akbar, Maharana Udai Singh II establishes a new Mewar capital on the banks of Lake Pichola. A sage, the story goes, told him this site would never be conquered. Construction of the City Palace begins.
1600s
Mewar Resists the Mughals
The Mewar Rajputs are the only major Rajput clan to never fully submit to Mughal authority. Maharana Pratap's legendary Battle of Haldighati against Akbar's forces in 1576 becomes the stuff of national legend, still celebrated today.
1818
Treaty with the British East India Company
Mewar signs a treaty with the British, becoming a "princely state" under British suzerainty. The royal family retains symbolic rule but cedes sovereignty. The City Palace continues to grow under successive maharanas.
1983
James Bond Puts Udaipur on the Global Map
Octopussy — the 13th James Bond film — is filmed extensively at the Lake Palace Hotel and around Udaipur, bringing international attention to the city. The Lake Palace was literally built on an island in 1746 as a summer palace. Today it's one of the world's most photographed hotels.
